When the Logical Path Is Closed

We all make plans. Plans make life feel manageable; they give us the illusion that the future is something we can navigate with foresight. But the problem isn’t planning—it’s depending on the plan for our peace. Paul and Silas were moving with strategic intent toward Asia, a choice that made every bit of sense.

Plans as Identity

Over time, our plans stop being what we do and start becoming who we are. When a plan is interrupted, it doesn't just feel inconvenient—it feels personal.

The Illusion of Security

We think if we plan well enough, we won't get hurt. But security doesn't come from foresight—it comes from trust.

"Plans are good servants, but terrible masters. God isn’t against your plans; He is against anything that takes His place."

I. The Geography of the Funnel

Geography became a divine wall. To their left was Asia—forbidden. To their right was Bithynia—blocked. They were squeezed into the narrow corridor of Mysia, forced toward the Aegean coast.

The "Spirit of Jesus" Says No

Acts 16:7 is explicit: "the Spirit of Jesus did not allow them to go there." This tells us that Jesus is not just the fulfiller of promises; He is the Lord who directs paths. The same Jesus who saves us also stops us.

Not Every Calling is God's

Just because something has your attention doesn't mean it has God's assignment. Noise is not guidance. God pulling us away from a "good" thing is often an act of protection before it becomes an idol.

II. Troas: The End of the Road

Troas was the end of land travel. It is the place where all your skills have taken you as far as they can go. You’ve reached the limit of human control.

Pastoral Insight

"Troas is where God gently unravels us. Everything we thought was sure is laid down. It is where confidence turns into dependence."

"God doesn't compete with our plans—He patiently waits until they fall silent."

III. Vision and Redirection

God gives a call, not a map. In Troas, once the noise of their own plans died down, they finally heard God’s voice through the Macedonian vision. Obedience often requires trust before clarity.

From Strategy...

Paul wanted Asia—a broad Roman province.

...To Personal

God wanted Philippi—a specific riverbank and a woman named Lydia.

The Living Room Circle

Discussion & Reflection

01

Which part of your life right now feels like a "Red Light"? How are you managing the feeling that God might be stopping you rather than just your plan?

02

"Noise is not guidance." What are the voices (internal or external) currently competing for your attention that might be drowning out the Spirit?

03

Have you reached a "Troas"—the end of your skill set or your ability to fix a situation? What is the hardest part of being still there?

04

Family Prompt: When the GPS in the car suddenly changes our route, how do we feel? How can we trust God's "GPS" as a family when our plans for the week get changed?

05

Redirecting Paul led him to Lydia’s house. Who in your life might God be leading you toward while you are focused on your bigger "strategic" goals?
